About Art

There's something meditative about drawing. The act doesn't simply calm, but gives me space to describe and work through emotions I would otherwise leave alone. Admittedly, much of my work is practice, exploring technique rather than concepts, but I believe that's also interesting.

I first learned to draw by mimicking anime, so I often slip into that style when not paying attention. My other main inspiration is my twin, who, in our mutual quest to differentiate our artistic voices, has helped me more than anyone else.

Artist Statement

School haunts me. Its rigid demands of intellect, order, and obedience loomed above my adolescence, yet its promises were saccharine. Its pseudo-objective, concrete standards of worth mesmerized me. I dedicated my childhood to its cold tasks of logic, hardly noticing as my routines became mechanical and spaces, spartan. Even my exhaustion became a cracked mirror of academia, merely an abstract meter of daily productivity. Perfection was the game, and I would win. In that fervor, something shifted—something fundamental to my being and something I canâ€™t identify. In this series of pencil and pen collages, I seek to understand that change by juxtaposing my homework with self-perception as I consider various aspects of my relationship with work.
